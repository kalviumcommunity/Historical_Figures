Albert Einstein:
A theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity. Born in 1879 in Germany, he made groundbreaking contributions to theoretical physics and won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921.

Marie Curie:
A physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. Born in 1867 in Poland, she was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences.

APJ Abdul Kalam:
An aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man of India," he was instrumental in India's missile development and inspired millions through his books and speeches.

Abraham Lincoln:
The 16th President of the United States, serving from 1861 to 1865. He led the nation during the American Civil War and is remembered for abolishing slavery through the Emancipation Proclamation.

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj:
A 17th-century Indian warrior king and the founder of the Maratha Empire. Known for his innovative military tactics, administrative reforms, and promotion of regional languages and culture.
